### Target Users
- Gen Z and younger
- Students high school and above
  - Overwhelmed with school work
  - Neurodivergent, ADHD (feeling overwhelmed with too much work at once)
- Lonely
- Startup founders
- Engineers
- Artists

### Functional Requirements
- There shall be an onboarding process consisting of multiple choice questions 
  - Multiple choice questions shall narrow down the type of user the user is
- There shall be a chart tracking AI usage
  - There shall be filters by...
    - Name of AI application
    - By month
    - By week
- The app shall allow the user to set goals on number of AI usages (ChatGPT, Gemini, Claude, etc.)
  - The app shall prompt the user to justify AI usage after number of uses (specified in goal) are exhausted
    - Multiple choice questions (choices generated by AI) shall be provided to ask the user about their planned usage of AI
    - There shall be a catch-all text-based input in addition to each multiple choice question.
    - Responses shall be fed to an AI model to help the user decide how rational AI usage is.
    - User shall be given an alternative course of action if AI usage is rejected
    - There shall be a maximum of 5 questions asked.
  - The app shall enforce a time limit on the AI use (e.g. 30 min each "free" time)
- The app shall provide accessibility
  - The app shall provide text-to-speech
  - The app shall provide tab-focus functionality
- The app shall give authentication

### Pain Points for AI
- Using AI on small tasks repeatedly, then continuing use into harder tasks, increasing dependency in the future on the same task
    - Snowball effect of using AI
- Wishes to use AI less to learn more (from inexperienced developer)
    - Aidan:
        - Tried to use AI to learn MIPS assembly due to feeling overwhelmed and having other assignments
          Benefits of AI
- Memory in AI is limited; won't last for long-term social activities (e.g. relationships, mentorship, "socializing")
  - This leads to a false, precarious sense of security
